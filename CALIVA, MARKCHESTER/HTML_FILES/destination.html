Culture

La Union's culture is a rich tapestry blending indigenous Ilocano traditions with influences from Spanish and American colonizers. Its vibrant heritage is reflected in traditional practices like 'inabel' weaving and folk dances. The province celebrates various festivals, including the "Sillag: Festival of Lights," showcasing its tourism and agri-industrial products. Culinary delights range from seafood dishes to Ilocano specialties like pinakbet and bagnet. The arts and crafts scene thrives with handwoven products, pottery, and woodcarvings depicting local folklore. Catholicism is prominent, with historic Spanish colonial churches serving as cultural landmarks, while the province's emerging surfing culture adds a youthful and dynamic vibe to its cultural landscape.
